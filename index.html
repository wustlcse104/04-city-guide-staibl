Harvard Square

Mission Statement

After living in Harvard Square for years, I have become well-versed in the tourist traps and overall places to avoid. In making this website, I hope to save you the embarassment of asking unfriendly, hipster New Englanders for directions. Use this as not only a map to help you navigate your way around Harvard Square, but also a travel book showing you where you should visit. I've highlighted my favorite clothing store, coffee places and cafes in the area.

Places of Interest

Zinneken's Belgian Waffles

1154 Massachusetts Ave, Cambridge, MA 02138

Zinneken's is an independently owned bistro that has scrumptious, nutella-topped waffles along with delicious coffee. Located right down the street from Harvard Yard, it is the perfect place to replenish after shopping or sight seeing. While already delicious on its own, you can add a variety of toppings to the waffles. Below is a list of the toppings you can add, along with their prices.

Topping Price
Chocolate or Nutella $1
Whipped Cream $1
Ice Cream $2.50
Fruit $1per fruit

Tatte Bakery

1288 Massachusetts Ave, Cambridge, MA 02138

A trendy bakery that is the perfect alternative when you are sick of Panera, having even more delicious breads and pasteries. This is also an essential if your Instagram is lacking a brunch post.

Felipe's Taqueria

21 Brattle St, Cambridge, MA 02138

Felipe's is a Mexican restaurant with a rooftop bar, making it the perfect spot for the summer. Don't leave without getting extra guac to take home!

Crema Cafe

27 Brattle St, Cambridge, MA 02138

Another independently owned establishment, Crema sells unique seasonal drinks along with an assortment of paninis.

LF Stores

26 Church St, Cambridge, MA 02138
